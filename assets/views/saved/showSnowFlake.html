The Snowflake Method

Feeling ambitious?

Writing a novel? Novella? Short story? The snowflake method is designed to help you expand a single sentence into a full story.

Directions:

1.Take your time and write a one-sentence summary of your story below. Keep it simple and concise

2.Expand your sentence into a paragraph describing the story narrative, any major events and the ending.

3.Now consider the main character and write a one page summary for each, considering the following points:

  1. A one-sentence summary of the character's storyline.
  2. The character's motivation (what does he/she want abstractly?)
  3. The character's goal (what does he/she want concretely?)
  4. The character's conflict (what prevents him/her from reaching this goal?)
  5. The character's epiphany (what will he/she learn, how will he/she change?
  6. A one-paragraph summary of the character's storyline

4.Go back to #2 and expand each sentence into a paragraph

5.Write a one page description for each major character, which tells the story from their point of view.

6.Expand your one page plot synopsis into a four page plot synopsis.

7.Using the expanded synopsis, make a list of every scene you will need to write to complete the novel.

8.Using the scene list, write a multi-paragraph narrative description of each scene.

9.Write your first draft
